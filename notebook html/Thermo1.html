Part I: Introduction - States of Matter and Pressure

1. Definitions and Overview

This section provides a brief overview of key concepts in thermodynamics.

1.0.1 Terminology

  • Isolated system: A system where neither heat nor matter is exchanged with the surroundings.
  • Adiabatic walls: Heat-insulating walls, meaning no heat exchange \(\Delta Q=0\).
  • Diathermic walls: Non-heat-insulating walls.
  • Quasi-static process: A process that is slow enough so that the system is always approximately in equilibrium.
  • Reversible process: A process that can be reversed by an infinitesimal change in conditions.

1.1 The Zeroth Law of Thermodynamics

The zeroth law defines the concept of temperature. Two systems in thermal equilibrium share the same temperature.

1.2 The First Law of Thermodynamics

The first law is the conservation of energy:

$$ dU = \delta W + \delta Q $$

The change in internal energy \(U\) equals the work done plus the heat added to the system.

1.3 The Second Law of Thermodynamics

Consider a system with half a container filled with gas and half a vacuum. When the partition is removed, the gas expands irreversibly. This leads us to define entropy \(S\), and the second law:

The entropy of an isolated system always increases \(\Delta S \geq 0\).

1.4 Pressure

Definition: Pressure \(P\) is the force per unit area:

$$ P = \frac{dF_{\perp}}{da} $$

Units: 1 Pascal = \(\frac{1N}{1m^2} = 10^{-5} bar = 10^{-5} atm = 1.45 \cdot 10^{-4} PSI.\)

1.5 Basic Concepts in Atomic Physics and Chemistry

  • Atomic number \(Z\): The number of protons in an atom's nucleus.
  • Molar mass: The mass of one mole of a substance in grams per mole.
  • Avogadro's number \(N_A\): The number of particles in one mole, approximately \(6 \cdot 10^{23}\).
